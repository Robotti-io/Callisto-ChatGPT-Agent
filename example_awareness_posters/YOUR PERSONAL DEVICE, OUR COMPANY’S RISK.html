YOUR PERSONAL DEVICE, OUR COMPANY’S RISK

Why BYOD feels flexible — and why it’s not

“I just wanted to check my work email on my phone — I didn’t think it could open the door to a breach.”

Using your personal device for work tasks offers convenience, but it also introduces risks to company data. Personal apps may seem harmless, but they can access sensitive information and compromise security.

What’s at stake when apps overreach

Consider these potential risks:

  • Data leakage: Personal apps may inadvertently access and share corporate data.
  • Malware infections: Unvetted apps can introduce malware to the device, affecting company networks.
  • Lost or stolen devices: Unauthorized access to company information if the device is misplaced.

Protecting both personal and professional data

To mitigate these risks, consider the following practices:

  • Use Mobile Application Management (MAM): Allows control over work-related apps without affecting personal data.
  • Install security software: Protects against malware and unauthorized access.
  • Regularly update apps and OS: Ensures the latest security patches are applied.
🔽 BOTTOM LINE:
Your personal device is a gateway to company data. Treat it with the same security considerations as any corporate asset.

"Convenience shouldn't compromise security."

App Management

  • 📱 Limit app installations: Only install apps necessary for work tasks.
  • 🔒 Review app permissions: Ensure apps don't have unnecessary access to sensitive data.

Device Security

  • 🛡️ Enable device encryption: Protects data in case of loss or theft.
  • 🔐 Use strong passwords: Prevents unauthorized access to your device.

Company Policies

  • 📄 Follow BYOD guidelines: Adhere to company policies regarding personal device use.
  • 🧩 Separate work and personal data: Use containers or profiles to keep data distinct.
