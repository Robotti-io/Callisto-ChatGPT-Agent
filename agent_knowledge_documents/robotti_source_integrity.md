# 🛡️ Source Integrity Guide

Callisto’s trust is built on more than knowledge — it’s built on source care.  
Every poster must reflect real, verifiable links from credible organizations.

Citing isn’t about compliance — it’s about protecting confidence and calm.  
This guide defines how to cite cleanly, clearly, and with integrity.

---

## ✅ Approved Source Types

- Official orgs (e.g., [NIST](https://www.nist.gov), [CISA](https://www.cisa.gov), [OWASP](https://owasp.org))
- Platform-specific security hubs (e.g., [Microsoft Security](https://learn.microsoft.com))
- Reputable tech journalism (e.g., Wired, The Verge, Ars Technica)
- Research blogs from trusted vendors (e.g., Cloudflare, SentinelOne)

---

## 🕵️ Verification Rituals

Before citing a source, confirm:

- ✅ The link resolves successfully with an HTTP 200 status
- ✅ The content reflects the **exact** risk or example mentioned
- ✅ The link is stable (not behind auth, not a redirect trap)
- ❌ No placeholder or fake URLs (e.g., `/example-link`)
- ❌ No homepage-only links — always link directly to relevant articles

🚫 Also confirm the article is **appropriate for a general workplace audience**:

- ❌ Avoid sources that include sexually explicit content, graphic violence, or emotionally charged headlines
- ❌ Avoid citations from articles that could be viewed as NSFW, distressing, or polarizing
- ✅ Prefer sources that are neutrally written and broadly accessible

🛡️ When in doubt:

- Look for a neutral-phrased version of the story from the same or similar source
- Focus on behavior, not headlines

---

## 🏷️ Citation Practice

- Use **2–3 citations max** per poster
- Embed links directly into the **“Further Reading”** section — using real, full URLs (no markdown-style links)
- Use **neutral phrasing**:  
  - ✅ “According to NIST’s AI Framework…”  
  - ❌ “Experts say…”  
- Cite calm, not fear: the goal is context, not alarm

---

## 🚩 Flagging Unverified Claims

If a specific fact or stat cannot be traced to a trustworthy source:

- Insert: `<!-- VERIFY FACT -->`
- Or rephrase to focus on behavior, not speculation
